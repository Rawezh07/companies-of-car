Ford

Ford Motor Company is an American multinational automaker that was founded by Henry Ford on June 16, 1903. It played a pivotal role in the development of the automotive industry and the assembly line production method. Ford is known for popularizing affordable automobiles and making cars accessible to the general public. The company's iconic vehicles include the Ford Model T, which became the first mass-produced car. Over the years, Ford has expanded its product line to include a wide range of vehicles, from trucks and SUVs to electric and hybrid models. The company is headquartered in Dearborn, Michigan, and remains one of the major players in the global automotive industry.
